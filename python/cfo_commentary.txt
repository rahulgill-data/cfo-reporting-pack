BOOKING HOLDINGS — CFO MONTHLY COMMENTARY
Period: YTD Jan–Sep-2025

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Booking Holdings delivered YTD revenue of EUR 23,131M against a budget of
EUR 23,478M, a shortfall of EUR 347M (-1.5%). While the headline miss is
modest, it masks a more nuanced picture: Merchant revenue outperformed by
EUR 332M, fully in line with the strategic shift underway, while Agency
revenue came in EUR 732M below plan as the structural decline accelerated
faster than budgeted. Operating income of EUR 8,950M represents a margin
of 38.7% — below the budgeted 41.5%, but broadly acceptable given the
cost pressures from the Merchant mix shift. Year-on-year revenue growth of
+26.6% confirms the underlying business momentum remains strong.

2. REVENUE ANALYSIS

Merchant revenue of EUR 16,692M exceeded budget by EUR 332M (+2.0%), driven
by strong EU accommodation volumes and continued customer preference for the
prepaid Merchant model over the traditional Agency arrangement. This
outperformance is strategically significant: Merchant revenue carries higher
payment processing costs but delivers more predictable cash flow and greater
customer data visibility, both of which underpin the company's long-term
competitive positioning. Agency revenue of EUR 5,508M fell EUR 732M short
of budget (-11.7%), reflecting a faster-than-anticipated structural decline
as accommodation partners accelerate migration to the Merchant channel. This
is not a demand signal — it is a mix shift — and management should resist
the temptation to re-budget Agency revenue upward in the H2 refresh.
Advertising and other revenue came in EUR 53M above budget (+6.0%), a
pleasing contribution from KAYAK referral volumes and OpenTable subscription
growth that partially offsets the Agency shortfall.

3. COST & MARGIN ANALYSIS

Total costs ran EUR 448M above budget (+3.3%), driven by two principal
items. Performance marketing (paid search and Meta) overran by EUR 265M
(+4.6%), reflecting increased bidding activity in Q3 peak season to defend
market share against OTA competitors; at 30.3% of revenue this sits 1.8
percentage points above the internal 30% efficiency target and warrants
close monitoring through Q4. Payment processing costs overran by EUR 210M
(+9.3%), a direct and largely unavoidable consequence of the Merchant
revenue outperformance — as Merchant volumes grow, interchange and gateway
fees grow proportionally, and this cost behaviour was undermodelled in the
original budget. G&A came in EUR 122M below budget (-16.9%), partially
offsetting the above items; this underspend reflects the non-recurrence of
the Italian regulatory settlement provision that inflated the FY2024 base.
The net effect is an operating margin of 38.7%, 2.8 percentage points below
budget but still amongst the highest in the global OTA sector.

4. KEY RISKS & WATCHPOINTS

- Marketing efficiency: Performance marketing at 30.3% of revenue is above
  the 30% ceiling. If Q4 competitive intensity remains elevated — particularly
  around holiday and New Year travel — full-year marketing spend could exceed
  EUR 9.5B, compressing the full-year margin below 35%.

- Agency revenue acceleration: The -11.7% Agency shortfall vs budget is
  running materially ahead of the planned migration curve. If H2 Agency
  volumes decline at a similar rate, full-year Agency revenue could miss
  the annual budget by EUR 1.2–1.4B, partially offset by Merchant upside.

- Payment processing cost trajectory: At +9.3% above budget, payment costs
  are growing faster than Merchant revenue (+2.0%). The unit economics of
  the Merchant model need to be reviewed in the annual budget cycle to
  ensure the processing cost assumptions reflect current interchange rates.

5. OUTLOOK

Full-year FY2025 revenue of EUR 24,926M has already been confirmed per the
10-K filing, representing +13.4% YoY growth and validating the underlying
demand environment. For the remainder of the planning cycle, the priority
should be holding marketing spend within the 30% efficiency threshold in Q4
and ensuring the Merchant mix shift continues to be reflected accurately in
forward budget assumptions. The business is performing well on the metrics
that matter most — volume growth, Merchant penetration, and margin
resilience — and is well-positioned to deliver on its full-year commitments.
